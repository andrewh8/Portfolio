About Me

Software Engineer transitioning from engineering project management with 5 years of engineering experience, 9 months of programming experience, a bachelor’s in mechanical engineering, experience managing high-value customer facing engineering projects in a regulated industry, and certifications in software product management. I’m currently a project manager for custom, long-lead, and critical engineered products for subsea oilfield development projects directly servicing large international customers. My role is highly technical and I coordinate cross-functionally with multiple departments, working particularly closely with engineering.

Previous positions include serving as a technical lead on projects, managing product and project documentation, integrating engineering product updates with production, and detailed design work on products with 1000+ features in the design tree with a focus on proper product design and change management.

I'm looking to transition my 5 years of engineering experience into the field of software development and have learned React, Javascript, Node, Express, Java, Spring Boot, MongoDB, SQL, and other supporting technologies. My portfolio and github demonstrate proficiency with these languages and technologies as well as continuous growth and improvement. I'm excited to take the next step in transitioning my career.

I entered the field of engineering for the purpose of acquiring and skillfully implementing specialized knowledge to develop meaningful products. Software engineering has an advantage over mechanical engineering in that so much is open source. Mechanical engineering is a broad field and it can be difficult to acquire a particular skill or expertise without a masters degree or long-term experience in a particular industry. My resume shows constant growth and advancement, and I want the flexibility to grow as I desire, with access to information and the ability to practice newly acquired skills.

While my current position demonstrates that I am highly organized and a good communicator, allowing me to manage projects effectively, there isn't anyone at my company who wouldn't say that it's my technical nature and careful attention to detail that adds the most value to the company. I want to apply those abilities to develop skill and expertise that has value not only to a specific company, but that is valued across an industry. I find software engineering interesting and challenging, and I spend 4 hours of every night advancing my knowledge and skills.

My journey started with the lectures from Harvard's CS50 course. I quickly jumped straight into writing code, utilizing the integrated development environment on Freecodecamp.org. It wasn't long after getting the feel for programming that I recognized the need to build projects in a real-world environment to make the skills stick.

While self-taught, the path I've taken has been closely tied to the content on The Odin Project, as this free bootcamp lays out a clear path with good instructions and high-quality resources. It's entirely project-based, hence the 15+ projects on my github profile. Aside from the material provided directly by The Odin Project, I have utilized MDN, W3Schools, Udemy, Youtube, and official documentation.
